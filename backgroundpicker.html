Facts

  • Almost 40 species of dolphins swim in the world's oceans. Most live in tropical and temperate oceans in shallow waters, and five species live in rivers.
  • Dolphins are carnivores. Their prey includes fish, squid, and crustaceans. A 260-pound dolphin eats around 33 pounds of fish daily.
  • Dolphins can live up to 50 years! While the average lifespan is about 17 years, some live up to 50 years.
  • Dolphins help the sick or injured members of their pod, often tending to them with care.
  • Dolphins sleep with only half of their brain active to stay alert for predators and to continue breathing.
  • Dolphins are very friendly to both humans and other animals, often seen playing and interacting with them.
  • Dolphins have delicate skin but heal quickly, even from deep wounds.
  • They stick with their mothers for a long time, sometimes up to 8 years!
  • Dolphins can jump as high as 20 feet out of the water!
  • The "killer whale," or Orca, is actually a type of dolphin.

Questions

  • How many teeth do bottlenose dolphins have? They have 72-104 teeth, but they don’t chew food—they swallow it whole.
  • Do dolphins drink water? No, they get fresh water from the fish they eat.
  • Can dolphins see above the water? Yes! Their eyes are designed for both underwater and above-water vision.
  • Can dolphins see colors? Studies suggest they can see shades of grey and the blue-green spectrum.
  • Do dolphins have hair? Yes, but only at birth. The hair falls out shortly after.
  • Are dolphins fish? No, they are mammals. They breathe air, give birth to live young, and nurse their babies.
